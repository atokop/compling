 Mike E said


	August 25, 2011 at 1:53 pm 
There are many effects of underground testing (contamination of ground water etc.)
But to think that fracking can be a “cause” of a major earth quake (shaking the ground from atlanta to maine) is to depart from science. 
We know what the cause of earthquakes are (basic tensions — potential energy — built up in the crust by the relative movement of plates, and then suddenly released — as kenetic energy — when the plates slip). 
Mining and drilling at the surface does not (and cannot) cause the build up of the kinds of violent (and huge) energy forces needed to shake the eastern seaboard.
Earlier we discussed here on Kasama the problem of people trying to hype real problems by injecting pseudo-science and speculation. It is (imho) counterproductive. And often people writing these pseudo-theories, and those circulating really aren’t treating science (or politics) seriously… if an article seems to hype concerns that someone wants hyped, it sometimes gets a pass.
Put another way: It is often pointed out that the right works by hyping hysterial (and even) bizarre fears (immigrant crime, or coming UN takeover, or Obama’s secret Muslim religion). But is it ok for the left to hype fears in a parallel way? And why do it when the actual facts about reality are compelling enough?
